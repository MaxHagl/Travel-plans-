OPTION 01Tyrol · Tannheimer Tal → Lechtal

Tannheim & the Wild Lech

Two turquoise high-valley lakes, then a wild glacial river and Austria's most spectacular footbridge.

You start beside a string of deep-blue lakes ringed by limestone walls, then cross a single pass into the Lechtal — one of the last wild river valleys in the Alps, where the Lech still braids across open gravel beds. The marquee day is a quiet lake-to-lake hike; the encore is walking 110 metres above a gorge on a swaying steel ribbon.

The Route, Day by Day

Day 1 · Arrival

The lake at golden hour

Late-morning drive south; check in near the Haldensee by early afternoon. A slow lakeside loop or e-bike spin, coffee and cake on a terrace, then — if legs are willing — the Neunerköpfle cable car for a sunset panorama down the whole valley. Dinner in Tannheim.

Day 2 · The big day

Lake to lake, then over the pass

Start early — the Vilsalpsee road is closed to cars from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m., so drive in before then or ride the little Alpenexpress. From the lake, climb the west shore up to the Traualpsee and the Obere Traualpe, two more turquoise pools tucked under the Lachenspitze. Snack at the alm, then drive over the Gaichtpass into the Lechtal in the afternoon.

Switch hotels
Tannheimer Tal → Holzgau, ~45 min over the Gaichtpass. Evening walk to see the suspension bridge lit up above the village.
Day 3 · Encore & home

The bridge, the waterfall, the drive back

Morning loop out to the Holzgau suspension bridge and the Simms waterfall, with a stop at Café Uta. Lunch in Holzgau, then home via Reutte and the Fernpass (~3 h).

★ Marquee Hike

Vilsalpsee · Traualpsee · Obere Traualpe

A nature-reserve classic: well-marked paths from a car-free lake up to two higher tarns beneath sheer walls. Not technical, just a steady, rewarding climb — turquoise water at every turn, an alpine hut for cake at the top.

OPTION 02Bavaria · Berchtesgaden National Park

Ramsau & the Blue Ice

An enchanted forest lake and Germany's northernmost glacier — the wild corner of Berchtesgaden the tour buses skip.

Most visitors to Berchtesgaden queue for the Königssee boats. You won't. You'll be at the Hintersee instead — a glassy lake wrapped in the "Zauberwald," the enchanted forest — and climbing into the Blaueis cirque, where the Alps' northernmost glacier still clings beneath the Hochkalter. It's the most dramatic hike of the three, and the shortest drive from home.

The Route, Day by Day

Day 1 · Arrival

The enchanted forest & lake

Drive down and check in at the Hintersee by early afternoon. Walk the Zauberwald nature trail along the luminous, glacier-fed Ramsauer Ache to the lake; rent a rowboat or picnic at the painters' viewpoint where 19th-century landscape artists set up their easels. Quiet lakeside dinner.

Day 2 · The big day

Up to the blue ice

Early start from the Zauberwald car park. Climb steadily through mountain forest to the Schärtenalm (~2 h, ~750 m), then up into the Blaueis basin to the Blaueishütte at 1,652 m, ringed by the Hochkalter's walls. Pick your way over the boulders toward the glacier itself, then back down for the hut's legendary cake before the descent.

Switch hotels
Hintersee → Ramsau village, ~10 min downhill. Trade deep forest for the open meadow village and that postcard view of St. Sebastian framed by the Watzmann.
Day 3 · Encore & home

A roaring gorge, then home

A short, easy morning in the Wimbachklamm, where the river thunders through a slot in the rock on railed walkways. Coffee in Ramsau, then a straight run home on the A8 (~2 h 45).

★ Marquee Hike

Hintersee → Schärtenalm → Blaueishütte → Blaueis glacier

Forest paths give way to a steady mountain climb into a high rock amphitheatre. Beyond the hut the route turns rocky and demanding, but you can stop at the terrace and still stand right under the ice. Real mountain fitness pays off here — and the views are the payoff.

OPTION 03Tyrol · Wipptal side-valleys

Obernberg & Gschnitz

A mirror-still lake under the pyramid Tribulaun, then a silent mill-village valley — the quietest, most romantic of the three.

Just south of Innsbruck, two side-valleys branch off the Wipptal toward the Italian border and almost nobody turns into them. Obernberg cradles a spring-fed lake so still the pyramid Tribulaun lands upside-down on its surface; the Gschnitztal next door is a hush of larch meadows, a rebuilt mill village, and a pilgrimage church on a high shelf. It's the longest drive — and the one you'll have most to yourselves.

The Route, Day by Day

Day 1 · Arrival

Over the Brenner to the lake

Drive down and into the Obernbergtal by mid-afternoon; check in at Obernberg. An easy late-day walk along the rushing stream brings you to the lake at golden hour — the Tribulaun mirrored in the water, the tiny Maria-am-See chapel on the shore. Dinner in the village.

Day 2 · The big day

Lake loop, then the next valley

Morning loop right around the Obernberger See beneath the Tribulaun — and, for stronger legs, on up toward the Portjoch on the Italian border. In the afternoon, a short drive over to the Gschnitztal.

Switch hotels
Obernberg → Gschnitz / Trins, ~30 min. Late-afternoon stroll to the Sandes waterfall and the rebuilt mill village at the valley head. Dinner in the quiet.
Day 3 · Encore & home

A pilgrimage church, then home

Climb up to St. Magdalena, the 1,660 m pilgrimage church on a meadow shelf with huge valley views (~2 h up). Lunch below, then home over the Brenner and down the Inntal (~3 h 15).

★ Marquee Hike

Obernberger See loop · beneath the Tribulaun

Wide, easy paths from the car park lead through larch meadows to the lake, then a marked trail circles the shore. Short and gentle as a loop — or stretch it into a moderate half-day toward the Maria-am-See chapel viewpoint and the Italian-border saddle.

If you're torn

  • EASIESTWant the shortest drive, the biggest mountain payoff, and the smallest bill? Take Ramsau — just be honest about the glacier hike's effort.
  • BALANCEDWant lakes, an easy-moderate hike, and a genuine wow moment to remember? Tannheim & the Lech is the crowd-pleaser.
  • QUIETESTWant to feel like you have the Alps to yourselves, with gentle walking and the most romantic lake? Drive a little farther for Obernberg & Gschnitz.

Whichever valley you choose, the plan is the same: drive in, climb something beautiful, and let the second hotel feel like a different country.

Before You Book · July Notes

Book the small places now. July is peak season and the best lakeside rooms and quiet pensions in these villages sell out early.
Austria needs a motorway vignette (≈ €12.40 / 10 days) for Options 1 & 3. Option 2 stays in Bavaria — none needed. For Obernberg you can use the toll-free B182 Brenner road and skip the autobahn toll.
Start the big hikes early. Alpine thunderstorms tend to build in the afternoon — and the Vilsalpsee road is car-free 8 a.m.–5 p.m., so beat it or ride the Alpenexpress.
Pack layers and proper boots. Even in July, mornings at 1,600 m are cold and weather turns fast — and lake water sits around 17–18 °C if you're brave enough to swim.
